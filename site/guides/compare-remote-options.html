The best choice depends on whether you prefer first-party integration, cross-agent support, open source, or control of the relay.

Decision summary

Pick the first-party option for a single-vendor workflow. Pick CC Pocket for one client across Claude Code and Codex, MIT-licensed code, optional relay self-hosting, and no CC Pocket account.

Choose CC Pocket if…

  • You switch between Claude Code and Codex.
  • You want to inspect the client, daemon, relay, and protocol source.
  • You want to run your own relay or avoid creating a separate CC Pocket account.
  • You need a phone, tablet, or separate desktop as the remote client.

Choose a first-party option if…

  • You use only that provider's coding agent.
  • You prefer the provider's integrated account and support path.
  • You do not need a self-hosted relay or cross-agent client.

What “remote” means here

All three workflows are designed around an agent process associated with your computer rather than a generic browser IDE. The implementation and trust boundary differ, so compare the provider documentation—not just the user interface—before choosing.
